The Journey: 1958 to Present

From Pioneer to Living Legend

1958

THE PIONEER ARRIVES

Migration from Nakuru to Bungoma

Long before the era of modern logistics, Sarah Hersi Ali saw potential where others saw distance. In 1958, she moved her young family from Nakuru to Bungoma, a quiet outpost in Western Kenya.

  • Family settles in Western Kenya
  • First business ventures begin
  • Vision takes root in frontier town
1960s

BUILDING THE FOUNDATION

New Nyanza established

The empire-building phase begins. Sarah establishes New Nyanza Wholesalers and becomes a major agent for Bamburi Cement.

  • Bamburi Cement agency secured
  • Hardware business expands
  • Property acquisitions during independence era
  • Community presence established
1970s

CROSS-BORDER EXPANSION

Trade networks grow

Vision extends beyond Bungoma as cross-border trade networks develop between Kenya and Uganda.

  • Kenya-Uganda corridor developed
  • Daughters trained in accounting and business
  • Cross-border supply chains established
  • Regional influence grows
1980s

THE IRON MATRIARCH

Widowhood and resilience

After losing her husband, Sarah becomes sole provider for six children. Rather than retreating, she expands.

  • Sole provider for six children
  • Business continues to grow despite personal tragedy
  • Family legacy takes root
  • Known as "Iron Lady of Bungoma"
1996

AMINA'S NEW CHAPTER

Strategic move to Uganda

After Amina's tragic loss, Mama Hersi's wisdom guides her daughter to Uganda—transforming grief into unprecedented opportunity.

  • Mama Hersi's strategic guidance pivotal
  • Amina closes Bungoma hardware store
  • Moves to Uganda with mother's business principles
  • New markets, new opportunities emerge
  • Legacy expands beyond Kenya borders
2000s-2010s

SKYLINE TRANSFORMATION

Kampala landmarks rise

Amina's empire reshapes Uganda's capital, honoring the foundation her mother built.

  • Oasis Shopping Mall opens (2008)
  • Woman Investor of the Year award (2008)
  • Laburnam Courts Apartments launched (2013)
  • Presidential recognition from Museveni
  • Moran of the Burning Spear (MBS) from Kenya (2013)
  • African Businesswoman of the Year (2014)
2016-Present

TRANSFORMING NORTHERN UGANDA

Atiak Sugar Factory

A US$120 million project brings the first major industrial development to post-conflict Northern Uganda.

  • 16,000 acres for sugarcane cultivation
  • 6,000+ jobs created (primarily women)
  • 5,000+ farmers in cooperative
  • Regional economic transformation
  • Women's empowerment at scale
TODAY

AT THE HELM

Still working daily at New Nyanza

Despite age and health challenges, Mama Hersi continues her daily discipline at New Nyanza. She is not a memory—she is present.

  • Opens New Nyanza every morning
  • Manages operations personally
  • Mentors next generation
  • Asha Hersi Foundation active
  • Legacy living and growing

Family & Lineage

The Branches of an Extraordinary Tree

The Industrialist

Amina Hersi Moghe

From Bungoma to transforming Uganda's skyline. CEO of Horyal Investments, builder of Oasis Mall and Atiak Sugar Factory.

Extended family legacy in public service. Served in Kenya's Ministry of Finance, bringing family values to governance.
